I am a Ph.D. student in the Data Mining Lab (DataLab) at Seoul National University, advised by Prof. U Kang. My research focuses on machine learning under label uncertainty and representation learning for graphs — spanning PU learning, link (sign) prediction, graph classification, and domain adaptation across graph, tensor, and speech data.

Research

Learning under Label Uncertainty across Modalities
  • PU learning for graphs. D-MVP (Disentangled Multi-view Feature Propagation, KDD 2025) for graph-based multi-positive unlabeled learning, achieving state-of-the-art accuracy on five real-world graph datasets.
  • Speech recognition under label scarcity. MOCA (PAKDD 2024), DoReMe (Interspeech 2024), and MOCA-S (PLOS ONE 2025) for semi-supervised and domain-aware speech learning.
Representation Learning for Graphs
  • Link sign prediction. PiGLeT (ICDM 2025) relaxes the fully-observed-label assumption of prior signed GNNs, reaching SOTA on seven real-world datasets.
  • Link prediction. PULL (AAAI 2025) avoids treating unconnected edges as true negatives via PU learning, reaching SOTA across seven datasets of varying scale.
  • Graph classification. TAG (Two-staged contrastive curriculum learning, PLOS ONE) learns representations at both node and graph levels.
Domain Adaptation
  • Meta-learning on tensors. Meta-P2 (Meta-PARAFAC2), a meta-learning-based domain adaptation method for irregular tensor decomposition.

Supported in part by XVoice: Multi-Modal Voice Meta Learning (No. 2022-0-00641), funded by the Korea government (MSIT).
